About

I study how AI methods can extract, structure, and interpret knowledge from complex academic materials. My work examines semantic extraction in scientific texts, multimodal analysis of figures and tables, and knowledge graph construction for large-scale scholarly communication analysis.

Within digital humanities, I explore computational studies of classical Chinese texts, with attention to stylistic patterns, semantic change, and the responsible use of AI in humanities research.

Research Interests

  • Scholarly knowledge extraction and representation with large language models
  • Multimodal understanding of scientific figures, tables, and technical diagrams
  • Knowledge graphs for scholarly communication and bibliometric analysis
  • Digital humanities methods for classical Chinese text analysis
