Yiqin(Miracle) Zhang

School of Innformation Management | Nanjing University

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Information Management at Nanjing University, specializing in Digital Humanities, Natural Language Processing, and Scientometrics. My research focuses on exploring how large language models (LLMs) and AI technologies can be applied to the analysis and organization of scientific knowledge, particularly through semantic extraction, knowledge graph construction, and causal inference in scholarly communication. I am especially interested in bridging textual and visual content from academic literature, aiming to develop multimodal frameworks that enhance scholarly information retrieval and interpretation.

My current projects involve extracting figure and table descriptions from scientific texts, modeling stylistic features of classical Chinese works, and evaluating the impact of AI on digital humanities research. Driven by the goal of improving access to and understanding of complex academic content, I hope to build intelligent systems that support researchers in navigating, analyzing, and generating scientific knowledge more effectively.
